Privacy Policy

We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains what data MoodMark collects and how it's used.

Last Updated: April 7, 2023

Introduction

MoodMark is a browser extension designed to track and visualize your emotional state while browsing the web. We believe in transparency and user control, which is why we've created this Privacy Policy to explain how the extension works with your data.

By using MoodMark, you agree to the data practices described in this policy. If you do not agree with these practices, please do not install or use the extension.

Required Permissions

MoodMark requires certain browser permissions to function properly. Here's why each permission is necessary:

storage

This permission allows MoodMark to store your mood data, settings, and preferences locally in your browser. Without this permission, the extension would not be able to remember your emotional patterns or settings between browsing sessions.

activeTab

This permission gives MoodMark access to the currently active tab when you interact with the extension. It's necessary for analyzing text input and typing behavior on the current webpage, as well as associating mood data with specific URLs.

tabs

This permission allows MoodMark to access information about open tabs, such as URLs and titles. It's needed to track which websites you're visiting and associate mood data with specific domains for analysis.

<all_urls>

This permission allows MoodMark's content scripts to run on any webpage you visit. It's necessary for monitoring text input and typing behavior across all websites to provide comprehensive mood tracking.

Information We Collect

MoodMark collects the following types of information:

  • Text Input Content: The extension analyzes text you type into forms, comment boxes, and other input fields to determine sentiment. This is processed locally by default.
  • Typing Behavior: Metrics such as typing speed, pause duration, and deletion rate are collected to infer mood from your typing patterns.
  • URL Information: The domain names and page titles of websites you visit are recorded to associate mood data with specific sites.
  • Manual Mood Tags: Any mood states you manually tag through the extension interface.
  • Extension Settings: Your preferences for how the extension operates.

What we DO NOT collect:

  • Personal identification information (name, email, address, etc.)
  • Login credentials or passwords
  • Financial information
  • Browsing history beyond the domain name and title
  • Information from secure (HTTPS) form fields
  • Screenshots or visual content from your browsing

How We Use Your Information

The information collected by MoodMark is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Mood Analysis: To analyze and determine your emotional state based on text content and typing behavior.
  • Pattern Recognition: To identify patterns in your emotional responses to different websites and online activities.
  • Visualization: To create charts and visualizations of your mood data over time.
  • Feature Improvement: To improve the accuracy and functionality of the extension.

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties. All processing is done either locally on your device or, if you opt-in, on our secure backend server.

Data Storage & Security

By default, all data collected by MoodMark is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage API. This means your data stays on your computer and is not transmitted elsewhere.

If you choose to enable server-side processing for enhanced sentiment analysis, some data may be temporarily processed on our backend server. In this case:

  • Data is transmitted using secure HTTPS connections
  • Text content is processed and not permanently stored
  • No personally identifiable information is attached to the data
  • Results are returned to your device and stored locally

You can export, import, or completely delete your data at any time through the Settings tab in the extension.

Third-Party Services

MoodMark may use the following third-party services:

  • Natural Language Processing Libraries: For sentiment analysis of text content.
  • MongoDB: If you opt-in to server-side processing, we use MongoDB to temporarily store and process data.
  • Chart.js: For creating visualizations of your mood data.

These services are used solely for the functionality of the extension and not for advertising or tracking purposes.

User Rights

As a MoodMark user, you have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Right to Access: You can access all your data through the extension's interface and export it at any time.
  • Right to Control: You can choose which tracking features to enable or disable through the Settings tab.
  • Right to Delete: You can delete all your data at any time through the Settings tab.
  • Right to Portability: You can export your data in JSON format and import it on another device.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons.

When we make changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy and notify you through the extension interface.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we are protecting your information.

Contact Information

If you have any questions, concerns, or feedback about this Privacy Policy or MoodMark's data practices, please:

  • Open an issue on our GitHub repository
  • Contact the developer directly through GitHub

We are committed to addressing any questions or concerns you may have about your privacy and will respond promptly.
